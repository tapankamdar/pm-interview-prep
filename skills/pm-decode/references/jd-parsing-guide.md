# JD Parsing Guide

Read job descriptions like a practitioner. The difference between a prepared and unprepared candidate is often whether they read what the JD *implies*, not just what it says.

## The Seven Signals

**1. Repeated themes**
If a JD mentions "cross-functional collaboration" three times, that's a primary evaluation criterion. Count frequency. Frequency = priority.

**2. Order and emphasis**
What's listed first in responsibilities? In requirements? First = highest priority in most JDs.

**3. Verb choices**
"Own" vs. "support" vs. "contribute to" — these signal the expected level of autonomy.
- "Own end-to-end" = full accountability, no handoffs
- "Lead" = accountable with resources
- "Partner" = influence without authority
- "Support" = contributing, not driving

**4. Required vs. nice-to-have**
The required section is the filter. The nice-to-have section reveals what a Strong Hire looks like beyond baseline.

**5. Between-the-lines signals**
Common translations:
- "Fast-paced environment" → understaffed or high-growth chaos
- "Ambiguity" → undefined role that needs self-direction
- "Stakeholder management" → political environment with competing priorities
- "Wearing multiple hats" → small team, broad scope, limited support
- "We're building the team" → you'll be early; norms aren't set
- "Drive alignment" → cross-functional friction exists

**6. What's conspicuously absent**
If a PM JD doesn't mention data/analytics → the team may be measurement-immature, or they assume it's table stakes.
If it doesn't mention technical depth → they may want a business-focused PM, or the eng team is strong and opinionated.
If it doesn't mention customers → this may be an internal platform role.

**7. The real question behind every JD**
Every PM JD is really asking: "Have you solved a problem like this before at this scale and in this context?" Map the JD requirements to that question.

## Role Type Signals

| Signal in JD | Role type inference |
|---|---|
| P&L, revenue accountability, GM | Business owner / GM role |
| "Define and build", 0-to-1 language | Product-building, early stage |
| "Scale", "grow", "optimize" | Scale PM — different skill than 0-to-1 |
| "Support", "customer experience", "deflection", "automation" | Operational / functional leader |
| "Platform", "infrastructure", "API", "other teams depend on" | Platform PM |

## Seniority Calibration Signals

| Signal | Seniority implication |
|---|---|
| "Lead a team of PMs" | Director+ |
| "Influence without authority" | Senior PM+ |
| "Executive presence", "C-suite stakeholders" | Director+ |
| "P&L", "business ownership" | VP / GM level |
| "Hands-on" or "player-coach" | Director who still ships, not an org-only role |
| "Define the roadmap" vs. "contribute to the roadmap" | Owner vs. contributor |
