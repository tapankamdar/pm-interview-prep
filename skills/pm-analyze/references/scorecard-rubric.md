# PM Scorecard Rubric

Detailed scoring anchors for each dimension. Apply these consistently across rounds for meaningful trend data.

---

## Product Judgment (1–5)

Measures whether the candidate thinks like a product person: user first, problem before solution, taste visible.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1 | Jumped directly to solutions. No user framing. Could have been written by anyone. |
| 2 | Named users but didn't show genuine insight into their motivations or tradeoffs. |
| 3 | Solid user framing with reasonable problem prioritization. No surprising insight. |
| 4 | Showed genuine product taste — identified a non-obvious user need or challenge, prioritized with clear reasoning. |
| 5 | The framing itself changed how you thought about the problem. Unmistakable product instinct. |

---

## Storytelling & Specificity (1–5)

Measures whether stories are grounded in real decisions with real details, or are framework-level abstractions.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1 | Generic answer with no specific company, metric, or decision. Could apply to any candidate. |
| 2 | Some specifics but relies on common phrases ("I aligned stakeholders," "I moved fast"). No texture. |
| 3 | Real details present. Story is believable and follows a clear arc. No earned insight. |
| 4 | Specific decisions, specific outcomes, specific numbers. Sounds like a real event, not a rehearsed template. |
| 5 | The story contains an earned secret — a counterintuitive insight that only someone who made this decision could know. Unmistakably this candidate. |

---

## Metrics Fluency (1–5)

Measures whether the candidate chose the right metric, not just named metrics.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1 | Named metrics without connection to the business outcome. "We tracked DAU and retention." |
| 2 | Named appropriate metrics but couldn't explain why they were the right ones. |
| 3 | Chose reasonable metrics with basic rationale. Didn't distinguish between leading indicators and lagging ones. |
| 4 | Chose the right metric, explained why it was the right measure of the right outcome, named what the proxies were. |
| 5 | Showed the evolution of their thinking — why earlier metrics failed, what led to the right one. The test: "Does improving this number make the customer measurably better off?" — they answered it. |

---

## Strategic Thinking (1–5)

Measures systems-level thinking, second-order effects, sequencing logic, competitive awareness.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1 | Tactical answer to a strategic question. No sequencing logic, no ecosystem thinking. |
| 2 | Identified the right priorities but treated them as independent rather than a system. |
| 3 | Reasonable strategy with sequencing. Didn't address competitive context or second-order effects. |
| 4 | Named the system logic — why these pillars compound, why this sequence. Showed competitive awareness. |
| 5 | Changed the frame of the question itself. Revealed a non-obvious structural insight about the opportunity or constraint. |

---

## Differentiation (1–5)

Measures whether answers sound like only this candidate could give them.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1 | Generic answer any prepared PM could give. No personal insight, no earned conviction. |
| 2 | Some specificity but relies on common frameworks or buzzwords. |
| 3 | Contains real details but lacks an earned insight or defensible POV. |
| 4 | Includes earned secrets or a spiky POV. Sounds like a specific person. |
| 5 | Unmistakably this candidate — earned secrets + defensible stance + unique framing that couldn't be templated. |

---

## Communication & Structure (1–5)

Measures clarity, pacing, and self-awareness about when to stop.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1 | Hard to follow. No clear structure. Ran significantly over or significantly under. |
| 2 | Understandable but rambling or choppy. Didn't signal when transitioning between sections. |
| 3 | Clear structure. Appropriate length for most answers. One or two that ran long. |
| 4 | Clean narrative arc. Knew when to stop and let the interviewer probe. Signaled structure without naming it. |
| 5 | Felt like a natural conversation, not a prepared answer. Structure was invisible. Perfect calibration of depth to context. |

---

## Overall Score Calculation

Average of six dimensions, rounded to one decimal place. Use this for trend tracking across rounds.

**Hire signal thresholds (calibrate to company archetype):**
- 4.0+ average: Strong Hire signal
- 3.5–3.9: Hire signal (with dimension notes)
- 3.0–3.4: Mixed — depends on which dimensions are below 3
- Below 3.0: No Hire signal

**Note:** Differentiation below 3 is a yellow flag regardless of overall average. At senior levels, generic answers are disqualifying even if everything else is solid.
