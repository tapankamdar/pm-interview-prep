# Stage-Specific Prep Document Formats

## Recruiter Screen Format

Condensed cheat sheet. The recruiter is running a 30-minute filter. Everything should be scannable in 2 minutes before the call.

### Sections

**1. Opening pitch** — 3–4 sentences. Thesis-first, not timeline-first. The problem you've been working on and why this company is the right place to work on it at scale. Never start with "I started my career at..."

**2. Likely Q&A pairs** — 5–7 anticipated questions with response language. Include difficult ones: short tenure, prior rejection at same company, domain gap. Practiced, confident answers — not apologies.

**3. 3-pillar strategy + 1-line** — L1 soundbite + L2 summary. Do not include L3 at this stage.

**4. 3 anchor stories** — One per prior company, labeled by which pillar they support. Not full STAR — just the setup and outcome. Enough to deploy fluently if asked.

**5. One question to ask** — The single strongest one that signals strategic depth.

**6. Day-of reminders** — 4–5 bullets. What to lead with. How to handle the most likely liability. What not to do. This is the only section the candidate reads in the 30 minutes before the call.

## HM Conversation Format

Deeper, conversational. The HM is deciding "do I want to build something with this person?" Not a filter — a fit assessment.

### Sections

**1. Interviewer decode** — Not a resume summary. A psychological reading. What their career arc tells you they value. What they've built and failed at. What kind of thinking they demonstrate publicly. The peer-level rapport hook that exists between the candidate and this person.

**2. What they're evaluating in N minutes** — Explicit list of what they're actually checking, stated in their terms. Derived from their background, not from the JD.

**3. Opening — 60 seconds max** — Different from the recruiter screen opening. Leads with thesis on the *problem*, not background. Calibrated to what this specific person cares about.

**4. Conversation angles** — Not a Q&A list. Likely *directions* the conversation will go and how to play each one. Include probes the interviewer is likely to make mid-answer and how to handle them.

**5. 3-pillar strategy** — Full L2 version.

**6. 3-year vision** — Separate from 3 pillars. The architecture shift the role enables, not just the near-term roadmap. Use a staged progression showing current state → Year 1 → Year 2 → Year 3 → end state. Each stage has a "from / to" framing and the key insight that drives the transition.

**7. Earned secrets** — 2–3 exact sentences to say out loud that make the candidate sound unmistakably specific. Earned convictions, not prepared answers. Statements that could only come from someone who made these specific decisions.

**8. Story routing by conversation thread** — "If conversation goes to X, deploy story Y." Table format for speed under pressure.

**9. Questions for them** — 4–5, with rationale for each. Label what each one signals. The Q&A is often the highest-scoring moment at this level — don't underinvest.

**10. Day-of reminders** — What to lead with. What to avoid. Which moment is the highest-leverage. Format-specific notes.

## Panel Format

Multiple interviewers, same day, different lenses. The coordination problem matters as much as any individual answer.

### Sections

**1. Panel overview** — All interviewers listed with their evaluation focus. "Vinod owns execution/delivery. Anna owns product craft/user empathy. Bhaskar owns technical depth." State it explicitly. If unknown, infer from background.

**2. Per-interviewer cards** — One card per interviewer. See card format below.

**3. Story routing table** — Single table across the full panel. Stories as rows, interviewers as columns. ✅ = deploy here, — = not applicable, ❌ = used in prior round (don't repeat same day). Prevents same-story collisions.

**4. Strategic moats table** — Why this company is uniquely positioned (vs. competitors, vs. building it at a startup). Proprietary assets, scale advantages, ecosystem integration, closed-loop feedback. Forces an answer to "why this company specifically?" that any senior interviewer is testing.

**5. One thing to frame proactively** — Single most likely liability and the assertive reframe. Not defensive — a genuine repositioning.

**6. Day-of reminders** — 90 seconds then stop. Let them probe. Q&A is the highest-leverage moment. Format-specific notes.

### Per-Interviewer Card Format

```
## [Interviewer Name] — [Title]
*Evaluates: [2–3 focus areas / competencies they own]*

[2–3 sentence background decode — what their arc tells you they value]

### [Predicted Question]
- [Beat 1 — 15–20 seconds spoken]
- [Beat 2]
- [Beat 3]
- [Beat 4]
- [Earned secret to land]

[Repeat for 4–5 questions]

### Questions to ask them:
1. [Question — with note on what it signals]
2. [Question]
3. [Question]
```

**Beat format rule:** Each bullet is 15–20 seconds spoken. 4–5 bullets = 90 seconds total. Stop there. The probe is where the score is earned.
