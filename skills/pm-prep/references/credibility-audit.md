# Credibility Audit

Run this audit on every origin story and every pillar before delivering prep output.

## The Find-Replace Test

Read each section and ask: "If I replaced the company name with a competitor, would this still work?"

- If yes → it fails. The section is generic and will signal to an experienced interviewer that the candidate did surface-level research.
- If no → it passes. The section is grounded in something specific to this company.

**Examples:**

Fails: "I'm excited about this company's mission to make [category] more accessible. I've always believed technology should help communities."
→ Works equally well for any company in the same space.

Passes: "The [X]% of customers who haven't adopted AI features aren't unconvinced about AI — they're unconvinced that this AI meets their bar. That's a trust problem, not a product problem, and it's exactly what I've been working on."
→ Specific metric, specific diagnosis, specific claim about why this candidate's background is relevant.

## The Specificity Gradient

| Level | Description | Pass/Fail |
|---|---|---|
| Mission alignment | "I believe in [company's mission]" | Fails — anyone can say this |
| Industry insight | "I think [industry trend] creates opportunity for [company]" | Borderline — too common |
| Company-specific problem | "The specific problem [company] faces is [X], which I can see in [metric/signal]" | Passes |
| Career arc connection | "[Company's problem] is the same problem I've been working on from [angle 1] and [angle 2]" | Strong pass |
| Earned insight | "What I learned from [experience] that directly applies here: [counterintuitive insight]" | Strongest |

## Pillar Credibility Check

For each pillar, verify:

1. **Grounding fact** — Is there a specific company metric, user signal, competitive event, or regulatory detail that surfaces this as a priority? If not, it's a generic priority.

2. **"Only you" angle** — Why does the candidate's specific background make them credible to execute this pillar? If any senior PM could claim it, it's not differentiated.

3. **Sequencing logic** — Why this pillar now, in this order? If the three pillars are interchangeable, the candidate hasn't thought about the system.

4. **Competitor awareness** — What are competitors doing on this dimension? A pillar without competitive context is incomplete at Director+ level.

## Origin Story Credibility Levels

**Strong:**
The career arc leads *inevitably* to this company's specific product problem. "I've been working on [exact problem] from [angle 1] and [angle 2] for five years. This company is the first place where that's the whole job."

**Solid:**
Personal connection to the mission with a clean bridge to the role. Authentic and vivid. The mission fit is real even if the specific product problem connection could be sharper.

**Weak:**
Personal connection to the mission's broader theme without connecting to the specific product challenge. Could work at multiple companies in the same space with a find-replace. Authentic but too generic.

**Failing:**
Generic mission alignment statement with no personal narrative or specific insight. "I'm passionate about [mission]" with no evidence.

## Audit Output Format

At the end of every prep doc:

```
**Credibility check:**
- Origin story: [Pass / Flag — one line]
- Pillar 1: [Pass / Flag — one line]
- Pillar 2: [Pass / Flag — one line]
- Pillar 3: [Pass / Flag — one line]
- Overall: [Ready / Needs revision — brief summary]
```

If any section flags, provide the specific fix required before the doc is considered complete.
