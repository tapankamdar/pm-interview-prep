# Deployment Depth Cues

Every strategy, talking point, and prep answer should have three depth levels. Going deeper than the context warrants signals poor read of the room — as damaging as being underprepared.

## The Three Depth Levels

**L1 — Soundbite (15–30 seconds)**
The one-liner. Deployable in any context, even when interrupted. This is what you say when you get 30 seconds, not 3 minutes. If you can't say it in one sentence, you haven't found the insight yet.

*Examples:*
- "Make support the lowest-cost, highest-trust learning engine."
- "Context over query. Authority over relevance. Action over retrieval."
- "From finding things to knowing things — that's the arc."

**L2 — Summary (90 seconds)**
The three pillars with their sequencing logic. Standard deployment for most conversations. Stop here unless probed.

*Rule:* 90 seconds then stop. Let them ask. The probe is where you score.

**L3 — Deep dive (3+ minutes)**
Full detail: specific company data, competitive context, execution plan, risk acknowledgment. Only on request or when the interviewer explicitly invites depth. Do not volunteer L3 unless they pull it.

## When to Use Each Level

| Context | Level |
|---|---|
| Recruiter screen (30 min) | L1 for all topics; L2 only if they probe |
| HM conversation | L2 as default; L3 only when they ask "tell me more" |
| Panel interview | L1 to open; L2 on follow-up; L3 only for the one topic they probe hardest |
| Written take-home | L3 is appropriate — they gave you the time |
| Casual networking call | L1 only |

## When Generating Prep Content

When building any prep document (strategy, pillars, talking points), always produce all three levels:

```
**L1:** [One sentence — the soundbite]
**L2:** [Three pillars with sequencing logic — 90 seconds]
**L3:** [Full detail — only share on request]
```

Flag L3 content with a reminder: "Have this ready but don't lead with it — let them pull the depth."

## The "Read the Room" Check

After any L2 answer, the candidate should read two signals:
1. Is the interviewer leaning in or has their energy dropped?
2. Did they ask a follow-up or move to the next topic?

Leaning in + follow-up = invite L3. Moving on = they got what they needed. Never extend to L3 because you have more to say — only because they want more.
